Ice and Iron — HP-Driven Dynamic Combat Audio System

Wwise × Unity

A combat audio system driven by HP and damage parameters via RTPC, delivering real-time hit feedback and evolving musical intensity.

System Implementation
  • RTPC (HP)→Vertical music layering with sidechain ducking (Wwise Meter): Drone and Lead layers activate at HP thresholds, ducking against each other dynamically.
  • RTPC (Damage)→Multi-stage hit feedback (Blend Container): hit sounds evolve from Normal to Broken to Final Blow.
  • State (Game State)→Global state switching: triggers full audio mute on win/loss resolution.
Design Goal

Make audio respond to gameplay values in real time, reinforcing the player's sense of combat intensity and tension (danger or near-victory).

RTPC-Driven Footstep & Ambience Interaction System

Wwise × Unity

An interactive audio system based on ground material and spatial distance, driving dynamic footstep and ambient sound behavior.

System Implementation
  • Switch Group→Physical material feedback: four ground materials (Wood / Grass / Water / Stone), each with 5 randomized variations to avoid repetition.
  • RTPC (Distance)→Spatial boundary warning: real-time parameter mapping based on player distance to zone edge, expressed through linear pitch shift on footsteps.
  • Spatial Ambience→Differentiated acoustic zones: ambient beds switch with terrain, using birdsong, wind, cave reverb, and water to reinforce each zone's acoustic identity.
Design Goal

Reinforce the player's spatial and boundary awareness through audio feedback, using footstep pitch shift as an intuitive distance cue.

Sword Combat SFX Design & Integration

Sword Combat SFX · Wwise × Unity

Sound design and engine integration for five independent slash animations, triggered precisely via Animation Events into Wwise.

Design Focus
  • Layering→Multiple source layers shaped with EQ and processed through a Ring Modulator for metallic character, then combined.
  • Variation→Each action's Whoosh and Impact includes two variations, randomized via Wwise Random Container.
  • Integration→Animation Event → C# → Wwise Event, synchronizing SFX precisely to animation keyframes.

Hybrid Synthesis — Wuthering Waves Hiyuki Skill Sound Redesign

Hybrid Synthesis · In-The-Box

An in-the-box hybrid synthesis sound redesign. Core materials (ice, bells) use curated presets; other elements (Whoosh, Ice Crack, Pulse Wave, Burst, Eject) are custom-modulated through Ableton Live's built-in devices and external synths. Layering combined with multi-parameter automation and filter sweeps shapes the pressure and physical impact of the ice skill across charge, launch, and burst phases.

Toolchain

Ableton Live (Built-in Devices) · Vital · Surge XT · OB-Xd  |  Footage sourced from official IGN gameplay coverage

Design Focus
  • Hybrid synthesis material→Blending custom Operator / Wavetable / Vital / Surge XT synthesis with presets to build a unified, crystalline ice-attack character with strong impact.
  • Spatial & dynamic balance→Glue Compressor, Saturator, and Reverb cohere the layers and unify spatial sense, while boosting subgroup saturation without masking high-frequency detail.

Medieval Battle Axe Sound Design

Medieval Battle Axe · Ref. For Honor

Using For Honor's audio aesthetic as a benchmark, deconstructing battle axe sound layering to design melee weapon physicality.

Design Focus
  • Material consistency→A shared metal base layer and harmonic content keep tone consistent across actions; a shared reverb layer unifies spatial sense.
  • Impact feedback depth→Multi-layer source stacking builds varied weight in heavy metal weapon impacts.
  • Motion & momentum→Automation and envelope trimming simulate the changing momentum and speed of the axe swing.

Action Scene Sound Redesign

Cinematic Sound Redesign

Reconstructed sound layering and spatial response for an action scene, reinforcing narrative weight and immersion.

Design Focus
  • Gunshot layering→Split into four frequency-based layers (Transient, Body×2, Tail); variations are created mainly by swapping the Transient layer.
  • Footstep layering→Neutral footstep base layered with multiple surface contact sounds (Grass / Dirt / Stone / Wood) and clothing friction, with multiple variations.
  • Spatial reverb→Reverb shifts between inside and outside the cave, alongside changing wind throughout.
  • Scene atmosphere→Metallic, high-impact gunshots, muffled fire with sparks, shifting wind, and interior/exterior reverb states build a dangerous, cold, vengeful tone.
